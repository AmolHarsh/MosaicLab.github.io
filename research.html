Mobile Health Sensing

Developing wearable and mobile systems to monitor health outcomes. Our work focuses on creating wearable devices that provide accurate, real-time health monitoring to support medical decisions.

  • OpiTrack: A wearable-based tracker for opioid use (IMWUT 2021).
  • BodyBeat: A system that captures non-speech body sounds for health monitoring.
  • "Mobile Sensing of Alertness, Sleep and Circadian Rhythm: Hardware and Software Platform" (GetMobile 2020).
  • "Respiratory Rate Monitoring in Clinical Environments with a Contactless Ultra-Wideband Impulse Radar-based Sensor System" (Hawaii International Conference on System Sciences 2020).
×

Cognitive and Behavioral Health

Exploring the interaction between cognitive processes and physiological signals. This theme aims to understand how behavior and brain activity influence mental health and cognitive performance.

  • "EarlyScreen: Multi-scale Instance Fusion for Predicting Neural Activation and Psychopathology in Preschool Children" (IMWUT 2022).
  • "Prefrontal Modulation of Frustration-related Physiology in Preschool Children" (Developmental Cognitive Neuroscience 2022).
  • "Effect of Sleep and Biobehavioral Patterns on Multidimensional Cognitive Performance" (JMIR 2021).
×

Digital Epidemiology

Researching health trends and disease outbreaks using digital biomarkers. Projects in this theme address syndromic surveillance and health monitoring in public spaces.

  • "FluSense: A Contactless Syndromic Surveillance Platform for Influenza-Like Illness" (IMWUT 2020).
  • "Can mHealth Technology Help Mitigate the Effects of the COVID-19 Pandemic?" (IEEE Open Journal of Engineering in Medicine and Biology 2020).

Signal Processing and AI

Leveraging AI and signal processing for ubiquitous computing challenges. This includes creating efficient algorithms to enhance image resolution and adapt models for diverse applications.

  • "Knowledge Transfer Across Imaging Modalities Via Adaptive Autoencoders" (IROS 2021).
  • "Hyperspectral Image Super-Resolution in Arbitrary Input-Output Band Settings" (WACI 2022).
×

Interaction Systems

Designing human-centered systems that integrate seamlessly with daily life. Our projects involve innovative technologies that enhance everyday interaction and usability.

  • "Calico: Track Based Interactive and Relocatable Wearables" (IMWUT 2022).
  • "MechanoBeat: Monitoring Interactions with Everyday Objects" (UIST 2020).
  • "NOSE: A Novel Odor Sensing Engine for Ambient Monitoring" (IMWUT 2019).
