Research Focus

The MOSAIC Lab is dedicated to advancing mobile and ubiquitous computing to address challenges in health, behavioral science, and everyday interaction. Our research spans a variety of domains:

1. Mobile Health Sensing

We develop wearable and mobile systems to monitor health outcomes, including physiological and behavioral data. Our notable work includes:

  • OpiTrack: A wearable-based tracker for opioid use utilizing temporal convolutional networks.
  • BodyBeat: A wearable system that captures non-speech body sounds for health monitoring.

2. Cognitive and Behavioral Health

We study the interaction between behavior, cognitive processes, and physiological signals to predict and analyze health outcomes. Examples include:

  • EarlyScreen: A multi-scale framework for predicting neural activation and psychopathology in preschool children.
  • Prefrontal Modulation: Research on frustration-related physiology in children with varying irritability levels.

3. Digital Biomarkers and Epidemiology

Our work in digital epidemiology focuses on identifying biomarkers for health trends and disease outbreaks:

  • FluSense: A contactless syndromic surveillance system for hospital waiting areas.
  • Structured Prediction Energy Networks: Joint prediction of cocaine craving and euphoria.

4. Signal Processing and AI in Ubiquitous Computing

We leverage advanced signal processing and AI techniques to solve challenges in ubiquitous computing:

  • Knowledge Transfer Across Imaging Modalities: Enhancing mobile robot vision using adaptive autoencoders.
  • Hyperspectral Image Super-Resolution: Improving imaging performance in arbitrary input-output band settings.

5. Interaction and Human-Centered Systems

We design interactive systems that seamlessly integrate with daily life, such as:

  • Calico: Relocatable wearables with precise locomotion tracking.
  • MechanoBeat: A system using harmonic oscillators and ultra-wideband radar for monitoring object interactions.

6. Multidisciplinary Research

We tackle problems that intersect multiple disciplines, including sensor design, AI, and behavioral science:

  • Nutrilyzer: A mobile system for characterizing liquid food using the photoacoustic effect.
  • NOSE: An odor sensing engine for ambient monitoring in kitchen environments.
